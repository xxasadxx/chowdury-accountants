Executive Summary
"a strong niche practice with excellent client loyalty and unusually high recurring revenue, but still materially under-monetised."

The Chowdury Accountants Ltd has built a real practice asset: 600 active clients, £314,000 restated annual revenue, and £19,579/month of recurring income. The client base is durable, referral-led, and rooted in trust within the South Asian business community — especially hospitality across Greater Manchester.

The core commercial problem is not weak demand, retention, or systems. It is pricing discipline. On the current base alone, correcting fees adds approximately £109,340/year with no new client acquisition required.

Five Strongest Findings

  • Recurring revenue of £19,579/month is top-quartile for a practice of this size.
  • Client retention is unusually strong; growth has been almost entirely referral-driven.
  • Defendable niche in hospitality and South Asian owner-managed businesses.
  • Net margin of 33.9% (before MD remuneration) confirms viability before any fee correction.
  • Infrastructure for the next level already exists: systems, trust, staff, branded office.

Five Most Important Risks

  • Ltd and payroll fees are below market — approximately £109k/year uncaptured.
  • Founder dependency suppresses valuation and operational scalability.
  • Client data is fragmented; phone coverage absent, email incomplete outside GC and payroll.
  • Practice is almost entirely compliance-led with little high-margin advisory income.
  • Loss of Hassan or Aminul would create an immediate operational pressure point.

The hospitality niche has compounding referral geometry

In most markets: one satisfied client → one possible referral. In a dense, trust-driven community network, referrals behave as clusters, not lines.

Restaurant owner → cousin opens takeaway → landlord owns 3 properties → landlord's brother opens off-licence → supplier opens distribution company. That is a network effect, not a referral. Once you dominate a niche like this, it becomes extremely hard for competitors to break in.

A formalised referral programme could accelerate new-client acquisition from 40 to 50–60 new Ltd clients per year — adding £50,000–£70,000 of additional annual revenue by 2030.
